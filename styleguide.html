Style Guide

Typography & components you can use in your essay

Headings & Paragraph Rhythm

Progress used to be a steady car on a long highway. Not glamorous, not fast, but reliable — the Civic of social change.

Then, sometime around the last decade, we slammed our foot on the accelerator. We wanted justice now.

Use bold sparingly for clarity, and italics for reflection or tension.

Gold Blockquote

We didn’t lose our morality. We overdosed on it.

Use for aphorisms or moral turns. Limit to 1–2 per section.

Small Caps & Highlight

remember this: speed without grip just spins wheels.

Small caps = quiet authority. Highlight = subtle gold underline for key lines.

Reflective Aside

Maybe the light we feared was only our own reflection.

Use for soft author voice or contemplative beats without a full blockquote.

Scene Break

When the Ferrari spun, the repo man of resentment was waiting.

Act II begins. Keep readers breathing with occasional breaks.

Asides & Micro Emphasis

(It’s not shame that divides us — it’s exhaustion.)

He arrived dressed in certainty, with the posture of a savior.

Cadenced Lists

  • Short beats for momentum.
  • One long sentence for depth and texture that carries a thought forward a little farther than expected.
  • One whisper.

Lists can reset attention—use sparingly.
